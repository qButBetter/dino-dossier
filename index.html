Spinosaurus

(Spinosaurus aegyptiacus)

Description

The Spinosaurus is a dinosaur from the Late Cretaceous Period. It lived around 99 million to 93.5 million years ago. The Spinosaurus belongs to the Spinosaurus Aegyptiacus species. Other relatives of the Spinosaurus, such as the Irritator and the Baryonx, belong to the Spinosauridae family as well. Most Spinosaurus skeletons were found around North Africa. Today, it's generally a desert, but back 93 million years ago, it was a vast river system and flourishing with flora. The main part of the identification of the Spinosaurus and its relatives is, of course, its spine/sail. In most media, the Spinosaurus' sail is very dull, but, many reasearchers believe that its sail was more colorful, most likely used for social interactions. It would have been used like a male peacocks' colorful feathers.
